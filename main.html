About the Book

The Story

Bridge to Terabithia is a timeless tale of friendship, imagination, and the bittersweet journey of growing up. Written by Katherine Paterson and published in 1977, this beloved novel follows Jess Aarons and Leslie Burke as they create a magical kingdom in the woods where they reign as king and queen.

What begins as an escape from their everyday struggles becomes a transformative experience that shapes their lives forever. Together, they face bullies, overcome fears, and discover the true meaning of courage.

"You have to believe it and you hate it. I don't have to and I think it's beautiful."

About Katherine Paterson

Katherine Paterson is an acclaimed American author who has touched millions of hearts with her profound storytelling. Born in China in 1932, she drew from personal tragedy to write Bridge to Terabithia.

The story was inspired by a real event: the death of her son's best friend, Lisa Hill, who was struck by lightning. Paterson wrote the novel to help her son cope with the loss, creating a lasting legacy that has helped countless readers navigate grief and loss.

Characters

Jess Aarons

A talented young artist who dreams of being the fastest runner in fifth grade. Jess struggles with his place in a family of sisters and a father who doesn't understand his passion for art. Through his friendship with Leslie, he discovers his own strength and imagination.

Traits: Creative, sensitive, loyal, brave

Leslie Burke

The new girl who moves in next door and changes Jess's life forever. Leslie is smart, imaginative, and fearless. She introduces Jess to books, fantasy, and a world beyond the ordinary. Her boundless creativity brings Terabithia to life.

Traits: Imaginative, confident, kind, adventurous

May Belle Aarons

Jess's younger sister who looks up to him and desperately wants to be included in his adventures. Though sometimes annoying to Jess, she proves to be observant and caring.

Traits: Curious, loving, innocent

Ms. Edmunds

The music teacher who recognizes and encourages Jess's artistic talents. She represents the kind of understanding adult support that both Jess and Leslie need.

Traits: Supportive, creative, inspiring

Janice Avery

The school bully who torments younger children. Through Leslie's compassion, we learn that even bullies have their own struggles and pain.

Traits: Troubled, defensive, vulnerable

Themes & Symbolism

  • The Power of Imagination

    Terabithia represents the transformative power of imagination. It's a sanctuary where Jess and Leslie can be themselves, confront their fears, and rule over their own destiny. The imaginary kingdom teaches them courage and helps them face real-world challenges.

  • Friendship & Connection

    The deep bond between Jess and Leslie transcends social boundaries and gender stereotypes. Their friendship shows that true connection is built on mutual respect, shared imagination, and acceptance of each other's differences.

  • Loss & Grief

    The novel honestly portrays the devastating impact of loss and the complex emotions of grief. Through Jess's journey, readers learn that it's okay to feel anger, guilt, and confusion when processing tragedy.

  • Growth & Transformation

    Jess's journey from an insecure boy to someone who can pass on Leslie's legacy of imagination represents profound personal growth. The story teaches that tragedy, while painful, can lead to maturity and understanding.

  • The Bridge (Symbol)

    The rope bridge to Terabithia symbolizes the transition from childhood to maturity, from safety to adventure, and from life to death. Its breaking represents the fragility of life and the sudden end of innocence.

  • Nature as Sanctuary

    The forest and creek serve as an escape from the harsh realities of poverty, school pressures, and family tensions. Nature in the novel is both beautiful and dangerous, nurturing and unforgiving.

Fun Facts & Trivia

Based on True Events

Katherine Paterson wrote the book to help her son David cope with the death of his friend Lisa Hill, who was struck by lightning at age eight.

Award-Winning Classic

The novel won the prestigious Newbery Medal in 1978 and has been translated into more than 20 languages.

Film Adaptation

The 2007 Disney film featured visual effects and brought Terabithia to life on screen, though some fans prefer the book's subtler approach to imagination.

Banned & Challenged

Despite its acclaim, the book has been challenged in schools for its themes of death and use of the word "Lord" outside of prayer.

Inspiration from Many Sources

Paterson drew inspiration from C.S. Lewis's Narnia series, her missionary childhood, and her observations of rural Virginia life.
